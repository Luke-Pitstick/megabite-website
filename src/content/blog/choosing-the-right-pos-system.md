Your POS system is the heart of your restaurant's operations. Yet many restaurant owners choose based on price or sales pitch rather than actual business needs. Let's change that.

## Beyond Processing Payments

Modern POS systems do far more than just process transactions. They should:

- Track inventory in real-time
- Manage staff schedules and labor costs
- Generate actionable business intelligence
- Integrate with online ordering and delivery platforms
- Support customer loyalty programs
- Provide detailed reporting and analytics

## Key Features to Prioritize

### 1. Integration Capabilities

Your POS needs to play nice with:
- Online ordering platforms
- Inventory management systems
- Accounting software
- Reservation systems
- Delivery service providers

**Red flag**: If a vendor says "we do everything you need," be skeptical. Best-in-class solutions often come from specialized providers working together.

### 2. True Cloud-Based Architecture

Not all "cloud" POS systems are created equal. True cloud-based systems:
- Update automatically with new features
- Allow access from anywhere
- Provide real-time data sync across locations
- Have reliable uptime and disaster recovery

### 3. Flexibility and Customization

Can you:
- Customize menu layouts and modifiers?
- Create custom reports?
- Adjust workflows to match your operations?
- Add or remove features as needed?

### 4. Hardware Considerations

- **Durability**: Restaurant environments are tough on equipment
- **Flexibility**: Tablet-based systems offer mobility; traditional terminals offer ruggedness
- **Cost**: Factor in ongoing hardware replacement and support

## Hidden Costs to Watch For

The monthly subscription fee is just the beginning. Consider:

- **Hardware costs**: Initial purchase and ongoing replacements
- **Payment processing fees**: Often 2.5-3.5% per transaction
- **Support and training**: Some charge extra for phone support
- **Integration fees**: Connecting to other systems can add up
- **Contract terms**: Month-to-month vs. long-term commitments

## Top Considerations by Restaurant Type

### Quick Service / Fast Casual
- Fast order entry and modification
- Kitchen display system integration
- Mobile ordering support
- Speed of service tracking

### Full Service / Fine Dining
- Table management and server assignment
- Course timing and coursing
- Wine list management
- Tip pooling and distribution

### Coffee Shops / Bakeries
- Batch prep and production tracking
- Ingredient-level inventory
- Customer loyalty programs
- Gift card management

## The Migration Challenge

Switching POS systems is painful but sometimes necessary. Plan for:

- **Data migration**: Menu items, pricing, customer data
- **Staff training**: Budget 10-20 hours per employee
- **Downtime**: Have a backup plan for the transition period
- **Overlap period**: Run systems in parallel when possible

## Questions to Ask Vendors

1. What does the total cost of ownership look like over 3 years?
2. How do you handle system updates and downtime?
3. What happens to our data if we leave?
4. How long does customer support take to respond?
5. Can we see references from similar restaurants?
6. What integrations do you offer, and how do they work?

## Making the Right Choice

The "best" POS system is the one that:
- Fits your specific operation and workflow
- Integrates with your other essential tools
- Scales with your growth plans
- Provides reliable support when issues arise
- Delivers ROI through efficiency and insights
